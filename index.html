The Alliances

Tinig ng Silangan

With the goal of enlisting youth participation to overthrow the Marcos dictatorship, the Tinig Ng Silangan Party Alliance (TNSPA) was established in 1981 as a coalition of student organizations and fraternities. Almost four decades since its inception, TNSPA gave birth to Tinig ng Silangan Inc., a Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC)-a registered, non-stock, non-profit corporation, to expand its community involvement.

Nagkakaisang Tugon

The Nagkakaisang Tugon Party Alliance (NTPA), founded by David Victor Felix, is anchored on two tenets: Authentic Christian Humanism and Active Non-Violence. The NTPA originally donned the white-green color combination until the party made a surprising move in 2022, changing its colors to white and gold.

Your vote ensures that your voice and interests are represented in decision-making processes within the school. By voting for candidates who align with your values and priorities, you contribute to the selection of individuals who will advocate for issues important to you.

1. Login: Log in to your school's Canvas portal using your student credentials.
2. Navigate to Voting Section: Once logged in, navigate to the "Voting" section or tab. This could be located under a specific course or in a separate module dedicated to elections.
3. View Candidates: In the "Voting" section, you'll see a list of candidates running for various positions on the school council. Each candidate may have a brief profile or statement outlining their platform and goals.
4. Select Your Candidates: Carefully review the candidates' profiles and choose the ones you wish to vote for. You may be able to vote for multiple candidates for different positions, depending on the election rules.
5. Submit Your Vote: After selecting your preferred candidates, click on the "Submit Vote" button or similar prompt to finalize your choices.
6. Confirmation: Once you've submitted your vote, you should receive a confirmation message indicating that your vote has been successfully cast.

When deciding who to vote for in a school council election, it's essential to consider candidates' platforms, qualifications, values alignment, track records, vision for the future, community feedback, and personal intuition. Look for candidates with clear goals, relevant experience, and a commitment to serving the school community. Consider how closely their values align with your own and whether their vision for the future resonates with you. Additionally, take into account feedback from peers and teachers, but ultimately trust your instincts in making the decision. Your vote is an opportunity to elect individuals who will effectively represent your interests and contribute positively to the school community.
